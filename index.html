Disaster

  • Disaster ("bad Star" in Greek)
  • Consequence of inappropriately managed risk
  • Risk = Hazards + Vulnerability
  • Developing/low-income countries at high risk

Picture in Numbers

  • More than 95% of total deaths occur in developing countries
  • Losses are 20 times greater in developing countries

More Data

  • 1995 - 2014: 89% of storm-related fatalities in lower-income countries with just 26% of total storms
  • Past 30 years: > 2.5 million people and ~ $4 trillion lost
  • 1980s to last decade: Global losses quadrupling from $50B p.a. to $200B p.a.
  • 2017: $330 billion global losses

Credit: DRM Portal, The World Bank

April 2015 Nepal Earthquake

  • 7.8 Magnitude -- Mercalli Intensity of VII (severe) -- Barpak, Gorkha
  • Worst natural disaster since 1934 Nepal-Bihar earthquake
  • ~ 9000 casulties and ~ 22000 injuries
  • Avalanche in Mt. Everest (21 deaths) and Langtang valley (250 missing)
  • Major aftershock: 7.3 magnitude on 12th May (~ 200 deaths and ~ 2000 injuries)
  • Economic loss: 9 to 50% of GDP with best guess of 35% (USGS)
  • $6,695 M needed to rebuild and recover; ~ half of that cost stemming from housing ($3,278 million)

Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence

  • Machine Learning: Subset of AI, A technique for AI
  • Part of computer science since the late 1950s
  • ML: Computer programs that learn to perform specific tasks based on data inputs or rules
  • Supervised (labelled data) and Unsupervised (Statistical methods to group data with similar characteristics)
